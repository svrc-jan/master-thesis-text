\section{Motivation}

Railway transpotation remains relevent even after its over 200 year history due to its efficiency, safety, and sustainability, however the most limiting factor has always been the use of the required infrastructure. As such the critical problem is always been scheduling. The complexity of this problem is increasing with size and intricacy of the railway system as well as the demand to use it. 

Traditionally scheduling was done manually by humans, only with the advancements of optimizing algorithms and computational power are humans aided by computers. This still produces a static schedule that might be unusable if a disruption occurs. This is where train dispatching comes to play where the decisions are done in real-time, managing delays and conflics as they happen - this part is often still only done by humans.

The main goal of this thesis is to develop a heuristic that is capable of train dispatching in real-time with intepretable results - a human should be able to understand the structure of the result to verify its validity.

The heuristic will be evaluted \emph{DISPLIB: Train Dispatching benchmark library} {\cite{kloster2025displiblibrarytraindispatching}} consiting of train dispatching problem instances from real world data.
